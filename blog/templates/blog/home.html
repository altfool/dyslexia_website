Outliers

Unusual and noisy images can negatively affect voxel-based group analyses. A structural covariance approach that is packaged with VBM8/CAT12 toolboxes can help identify outlier images. In the manuscript below, we describe the identification of a case with Bilateral Perisylvian Syndrome with this structural covariance approach.

Eckert, M.A., Berninger, V.W., Hoeft, F., Vaden, K.I., Dyslexia Data Consortium. (2016). A case of Bilateral Perisylvian Syndrome with reading disability. Cortex, 76: 121-124. doi: 10.1016/j.cortex.2016.01.004.

Missing Multi-site Data

Missing data can be problematic for multi-site studies. Missing data can be imputed to enhance inference. In the manuscript below, we used multiple imputation to deal with completely missing data for a variable from one research site. We multiply imputed Verbal IQ across research sites and in this study showed that gray matter findings replicate in dyslexia studies when cases with low brain volume and low Verbal IQ are included in the dyslexia sample.

Eckert, M.A., Berninger, V.W., Vaden, K.I., Gebregziabher, M., Tsu, L., for Dyslexia Data Consortium. (2016). Gray matter features of reading disability: A combined meta-analytic and direct data analysis approach. eNeuro, 3(1), doi: 10.1523/ENEURO.0103-15.2015.

Missing data can also limit identification of unique behavioral profiles in heterogeneous multi-site data sets when a variable is missing that is critical for differentiating profiles. In the manuscript below, we tested different approaches for dealing with missing data to statistically estimate the missing data using a synthetic data set where we knew the reading profile for each case and then demonstrated the effectiveness of the methods with the real data (726 subjects).

Eckert, M. A., Vaden Jr, K. I., Gebregziabher, M., & Dyslexia Data Consortium. (2018). Reading Profiles in Multi-site Data with Missingness. Frontiers in Psychology, 9, 644.

Case-Control Advantage for Multi-site Studies

Research sites can have images from different scanners and different sequence parameters. This image acquisition variance, as well as difference is sampling, can increase noise. We used a case-control design to show increased sensitivity for observing brain structure differences between cases with different patterns of reading disability and controls. There were sub-cortical white matter findings across children with relatively a specific phonological decoding deficit, a relatively reading comprehension deficit, and generally poor readers. We would not have observed these significant differences with a multiple comparison corrected threshold using a standard group difference design.

Eckert, M.A., Vaden, K.I., Maxwell, A.B., Cute, S.L., Gebregziabher, M., & Berninger, V.W. (2017). Common Brain Structure Findings Across Children with Varied Reading Disability Profiles. Scientific Reports, 7(1), 6009.
